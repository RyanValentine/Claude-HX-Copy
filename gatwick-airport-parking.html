Gatwick airport parking — frequently asked questions

How much does Gatwick airport parking cost?+
Prices depend on the type of parking and how far in advance you book. With Holiday Extras, Park & Ride starts from around £4.24 per day when pre-booked, while Meet & Greet starts from about £6.25 per day. Booking in advance typically saves 50–75% compared with turning up on the day. Official on-airport Short Stay parking costs significantly more — from £13 for one to two hours.
Is it cheaper to book Gatwick parking in advance?+
Yes, substantially. For example, a week of Park & Ride parking at Purple Parking costs around £82 if you turn up on the day, but as little as £33 when you pre-book through Holiday Extras — a saving of nearly £50. The earlier you book, the more you save. We recommend booking at least 4–6 weeks ahead for the best deals.
Can I park for free at Gatwick Airport?+
There is no free long-term parking at Gatwick. However, the Long Stay car parks offer the first 2 hours free, which is useful if you are picking up or dropping off passengers. The designated terminal drop-off zones charge a minimum of £10 for up to 10 minutes. For the cheapest extended parking, pre-booked Park & Ride through Holiday Extras offers the best value from just £4.24 per day.
What is the difference between Meet & Greet and Park & Ride?+
With Meet & Greet, you drive directly to the terminal, hand your keys to a professional driver, and walk straight to check-in. Your car is parked in a secure compound and returned to the terminal when you land. With Park & Ride, you drive to a secured off-airport car park, park your own car, and take a free shuttle bus to the terminal (typically 10–15 minutes). Park & Ride is cheaper; Meet & Greet is more convenient.
How much is it to park at Gatwick for 7 days?+
A week of pre-booked parking at Gatwick starts from around £33 for Park & Ride and from around £50 for Meet & Greet when booked through Holiday Extras. Without pre-booking, a week in the official Long Stay car park costs around £56, and Short Stay costs considerably more. Pre-booking is always the best-value option.
Do I have to pre-book parking at Gatwick?+
You do not have to, but it is strongly recommended. Pre-booking guarantees your space (especially important during peak periods), and prices are dramatically lower. Holiday Extras car parks can fill up at busy times, and turning up without a booking means paying the much higher drive-up rate.
How early should I arrive at the car park?+
For Park & Ride, allow your normal airline check-in time plus 20–30 minutes to cover parking and the shuttle transfer. For Meet & Greet, simply arrive at the terminal at your booked drop-off time — the whole process takes just a few minutes. Shuttles run 24 hours a day, so even very early or late flights are covered.
Are the car parks secure?+
Yes. All car parks sold through Holiday Extras are airport-approved and feature robust security measures including 24-hour CCTV, floodlighting, perimeter fencing, entry/exit barriers and regular security patrols. Many also carry the Park Mark Award, a police-backed standard for safer parking.
Can I cancel or change my booking?+
Yes. With Holiday Extras Flextras, you can amend or cancel your booking for free right up to the day before your parking period starts. This gives you complete flexibility if your travel plans change.
Does Gatwick have electric vehicle charging?+
Yes. EV charging is available at Gatwick Short Stay car parks (22kW chargers), through the GRIDSERVE Electric Forecourt on the South Terminal ring road (150–350kW rapid chargers), and at selected off-airport providers such as APH Park & Ride, which offers 30–60kW charging during your stay. You can also pre-book EV charging with valet parking.
